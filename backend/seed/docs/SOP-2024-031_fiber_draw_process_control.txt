1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the process control requirements for optical fiber draw operations on Lines 1 through 4 in Building 7, Apex Glass Technologies Specialty Materials Division. This procedure applies to all draw tower operators, process engineers, and quality personnel involved in the production of single-mode and multimode optical fiber for telecommunications, medical, and industrial applications.

2. PROCESS PARAMETERS — CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS
The following parameters are critical and must be maintained within specified limits during all draw operations. Out-of-specification conditions must trigger immediate escalation per Section 6.

2.1 Draw Temperature
Target: 2050°C ± 15°C (measurement point: furnace centerline, zone 3)
Upper Control Limit (UCL): 2065°C
Lower Control Limit (LCL): 2035°C
Measurement method: Type B thermocouple, calibrated per WI-4398 quarterly schedule
Corrective action threshold: Two consecutive readings outside ±10°C of target

2.2 Draw Speed
Target: 22 m/s (range: 20–25 m/s)
UCL: 25.0 m/s
LCL: 20.0 m/s
SPC chart: Individuals + Moving Range (I-MR), plotted every 15 minutes
Out-of-control signal: Western Electric Rule 1 (single point beyond ±3σ)

2.3 Fiber Tension
Target: 50g (range: 45–55g)
UCL: 55g
LCL: 45g
Measurement: Inline tension gauge, calibrated monthly
Critical: Any reading exceeding 58g requires immediate draw stop. Peak excursions above 62g require NCR initiation within 4 hours and quarantine of affected spools.

2.4 Fiber Diameter
Target: 125.0 μm ± 0.7 μm (cladding diameter)
UCL: 125.7 μm
LCL: 124.3 μm
Measurement: Laser micrometer, continuous in-line measurement
Acceptance: Cpk ≥ 1.33 on a per-spool basis

2.5 Coating Diameter
Target: 245 μm ± 5 μm
UCL: 250 μm
LCL: 240 μm
Coating material: UV-curable acrylate, Supplier: PolyCore Inc. (SUP-004)
Coating concentricity: ≤ 6 μm off-center

3. STATISTICAL PROCESS CONTROL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 SPC software (currently OptiSPC v3.4) must be active and recording for all draw parameters throughout production. Manual entry is not permitted during active draws.
3.2 Control charts must be reviewed by the shift supervisor at the end of each shift. Trends (6 consecutive points moving in one direction) must be documented in the shift log.
3.3 ±3σ control limits are applied to all Shewhart charts. Out-of-control signals require a Process Deviation Report (PDR) within 2 hours.

4. OPERATOR REQUIREMENTS
4.1 All draw tower operators must hold Level 2 Draw Certification or higher (see TR-2024-015 training matrix).
4.2 Operators must complete the pre-draw checklist (Form QF-7041) before initiating any draw.
4.3 Unattended draw operations are not permitted on Lines 1 and 2. Line 3 and 4 may operate unattended for periods up to 30 minutes with active monitoring via the SPC dashboard.

5. PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE INTERFACE
5.1 Draw furnace heating elements: rated life 4,500 operational hours. Replace at 4,200 hours or upon evidence of degraded zone uniformity (temperature differential > 8°C between zones).
5.2 Capstan and coating applicators: monthly inspection. Any wear exceeding 0.1mm on capstan groove requires immediate replacement.
5.3 PM records are maintained in the Equipment Maintenance System (EMS). Operations must verify PM currency before starting any draw run exceeding 8 hours.

6. NONCONFORMANCE AND ESCALATION
6.1 When any critical parameter exceeds its UCL or LCL:
  a. Operator stops the draw or adjusts within their authority
  b. Shift supervisor notified within 15 minutes
  c. Affected fiber tagged for quarantine
  d. NCR initiated within 4 hours via QMS

6.2 Customer Impact Assessment:
  - Diameter excursion > ±1.0 μm for > 5 consecutive meters: notify customer quality within 24 hours
  - Tension peak > 62g: 100% of affected spools quarantined until tensile testing confirms compliance with IEC 60793-2-50 fiber strength requirements
