1. PROBLEM DESCRIPTION
On 2024-11-28 at 14:32, the SPC monitoring system on Draw Line 3 (Building 7) triggered an out-of-control alert for fiber tension. The I-MR chart for fiber tension showed a single point exceeding the UCL of 55g. The actual tension peak was recorded at 62g — 7g above the UCL and 12g above the target of 50g. Per SOP-2024-031 §6.1, the draw was stopped immediately by Operator T. Nakamura (Level 2 certified) at 14:35.

Initial quarantine action: 12 spools of single-mode fiber (Spool IDs: SM3-24-7201 through SM3-24-7212) placed on HOLD pending investigation. Estimated yield impact: $34,200 (12 × 1,500m spools × $1.90/m average price).

This event follows a similar tension deviation on Line 2 in March 2024 (CAPA-071, now closed). Root cause for CAPA-071 was capstan wear. Line 2 capstan was replaced as corrective action.

3. CONTRIBUTING FACTORS IDENTIFIED
3.1 Heating Element Degradation: Zone 3 furnace heating element runtime is at 4,200 hours against a rated life of 4,500 hours. Inspection indicates beginning thermal output inconsistency. Temperature gradient across the draw zone has increased from baseline ±3°C to ±7°C over the past 30 days.
3.2 PM Schedule: Preventive maintenance interval for heating element replacement was set at 12 months (approximately 4,500 operating hours). However, actual element degradation rate on Line 3 appears higher than the historical average used to set the PM interval, possibly due to higher draw speed utilization on Line 3 vs. historical Lines 1 and 2.
3.3 SPC Trend Undetected: Review of 30-day SPC data shows 6 consecutive increasing tension readings over the prior week — a Western Electric Rule 2 trend signal that was not acted upon at shift supervisor review. This points to a procedural gap in shift review follow-through.

4. ROOT CAUSE (PRELIMINARY — AI ANALYSIS PENDING)
Primary suspected root cause: Thermal gradient instability in Zone 3 of the draw furnace caused by end-of-life heating element, resulting in viscosity variation in the glass melt, causing tension excursions.

5. PROPOSED CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
CA-1: Replace Zone 3 heating element (Priority: Immediate, Owner: Maintenance, Status: Pending)
CA-2: Reduce PM interval for heating elements from 12 months to 10 months or 4,000 hours, whichever comes first (Priority: High, Owner: James Rodriguez, Status: Pending)
CA-3: Retrain all shift supervisors on SPC trend response procedures — Western Electric Rules review (Priority: High, Owner: Dr. Sarah Chen, Status: Pending)

6. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
PA-1: Implement predictive maintenance using Zone temperature gradient trending — alert at >5°C gradient (Priority: High, Owner: Engineering Team)
PA-2: Review all draw lines for PM currency against 4,000 hr threshold (Priority: Medium, Owner: Maintenance)
