NONCONFORMANCE REPORT
Document Number: NCR-2024-141
Title: Surface Defect — Gorilla Glass Lot 22B (Batch Materials Co.)
Status: Closed
Priority: Medium
Owner: Michael Torres, Supplier Quality Engineer
Department: Supply Chain Quality
Opened Date: 2024-09-15
Closed Date: 2024-11-20
Regulatory References: ISO 9001:2015 §8.7, SOP-2024-028, Customer Spec CS-DG-001

1. NONCONFORMANCE DESCRIPTION
During incoming quality inspection on 2024-09-15, Gorilla-type alkali-aluminosilicate glass substrates from Batch Materials Co. (Supplier ID: SUP-003), Lot 22B, were found to contain micro-inclusions visible at 40× magnification. The inclusions appear as small opaque particles (estimated size 5–20 μm) distributed across approximately 15% of the lot.

Specification: Zero micro-inclusions ≥ 5 μm per Customer Spec CS-DG-001 §4.2
Finding: Inclusions present in 9 of 60 substrates inspected (15% of lot)
Lot size: 240 substrates (200×200mm), all placed on HOLD.
Estimated rejected quantity: 36 substrates minimum (15% of lot)

2. DETECTION METHOD
Inspection performed by IQC Technician F. Guo using:
- 40× optical microscope (Olympus BX53)
- Transmitted light illumination for inclusion detection
- DIC (differential interference contrast) imaging for surface topography
Inspection scope: 100% of the lot was inspected for inclusions using the random sampling plan defined in CS-DG-001 Appendix A. Upon finding inclusions in the first 5 substrates, 100% inspection was activated.

3. DISPOSITION
15% of lot (36 substrates with confirmed inclusions) — rejected and returned to supplier.
85% of lot (204 substrates passing inspection) — released for production use after review by Quality Director Dr. Sarah Chen. A use-as-is deviation was granted under the condition that:
  a. 100% of released substrates undergo cleanliness verification per SOP-2024-028 §4 before coating
  b. Any substrate showing adhesion issues in coating is to be removed from the lot and traceable records maintained

4. SUPPLIER CORRECTIVE ACTION REQUEST
SCAR-2024-011 issued to Batch Materials Co. on 2024-09-18.
Response received 2024-10-05: Batch Materials Co. attributed inclusions to a refractory wear event in their tank furnace and implemented a furnace inspection protocol. No customer notification was made prior to shipping the lot (protocol violation).

5. IMPACT ON PRODUCTION
The 36 rejected substrates caused a production gap that was partially covered by safety stock. Net production delay: approximately 2 hours on 2024-09-16.

6. ROOT CAUSE (SUPPLIER-ATTRIBUTED)
Batch Materials Co. internal RCA: Refractory lining wear in glass tank furnace introduced zirconia particles into the melt. The affected furnace area has been relined. Enhanced melt inspection protocol (visual + transmitted light scanning of sampled substrate) was implemented by supplier.

7. CLOSURE STATUS
NCR-2024-141 closed on 2024-11-20 upon:
  a. Receipt and review of Batch Materials Co. SCAR response
  b. Two subsequent lots from Batch Materials Co. (22C, 22D) passing IQC
  c. No recurrence in 60 days
Note: CAPA-087 (Substrate Thickness Variation) remains open and addresses the broader supplier quality trend for Batch Materials Co.

8. CROSS-REFERENCES
- CAPA-087: Substrate Thickness Variation — Supplier B (root CAPA addressing broader SUP-003 supplier quality trend)
- SOP-2024-028: §6.2 references this NCR for substrate handling instructions
- AR-2024-Q3: Audit observation notes SUP-003 supplier performance trend as an area for improvement

9. LESSONS LEARNED
No supplier notification prior to shipment of a lot with known process anomaly is unacceptable. All Tier 1 suppliers have been reminded of the 48-hour advance notification requirement for any in-process anomaly per the Apex Glass Supplier Quality Manual §6.3.
