1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This procedure defines the cleaning sequence for glass substrates used in display glass and optical coating applications prior to anti-reflective and functional coating deposition. It applies to all substrate sizes from 100×100mm to 2200×2500mm processed in Cleanroom B (ISO Class 6) and Cleanroom C (ISO Class 7) at Apex Glass Technologies.

2. APPLICABLE MATERIALS
2.1 Substrate types:
  - Alkali-aluminosilicate glass (Gorilla-type, Supplier: Batch Materials Co., SUP-003)
  - Borosilicate optical glass (internal production, per WI-4420)
  - Fused silica substrates (Supplier: Silica Source Inc., SUP-001)
2.2 Cleaning agents (must be from approved vendor list AVL-2024):
  - Deionized water: resistivity ≥ 18.2 MΩ·cm at 25°C
  - Isopropyl alcohol (IPA): electronic grade, ≥ 99.9% purity
  - Semiconductor-grade detergent: Brite-Clean SC-45 (5ml/L dilution)
2.3 Prohibited: Acetone, HF-based cleaners, and abrasive pads are strictly prohibited on coated or pre-coated substrates.

3. CLEANING SEQUENCE
Step 1 — Pre-rinse
  Equipment: Ultrasonic bath, 40kHz, 60°C DI water
  Duration: 5 minutes
  Purpose: Remove bulk contamination, particulates, and residual oils

Step 2 — Detergent wash
  Equipment: Megasonic cleaner (1MHz), Brite-Clean SC-45 at 5ml/L in DI water, 50°C
  Duration: 3 minutes
  Note: Megasonic at 1MHz for substrates < 3mm thickness. Use 40kHz for substrates ≥ 6mm to prevent stress fracture.

Step 3 — IPA wipe (for substrates with operator-visible streaks only)
  Equipment: Class 100 cleanroom wipes (Texwipe TX4011), IPA
  Method: Single-pass horizontal strokes, overlap 50%. Never rub in circular motion.
  Applicable: Substrates with visible IPA rings or DI water spots after Step 2

Step 4 — Final DI rinse
  Equipment: Cascade rinser, 18.2 MΩ·cm DI water, ambient temperature
  Duration: 2 minutes minimum
  Flow rate: ≥ 5 L/min per substrate position

Step 5 — Spin-dry (for substrates ≤ 300mm)
  Equipment: Semitool ST-200 spin dryer
  Speed: 1500 RPM, 60 seconds
  For substrates > 300mm: Nitrogen blow-off gun, ULPA-filtered N2

4. CLEANLINESS VERIFICATION
4.1 Mandatory inspection after cleaning, before coating:
  - Particle count: ≤ 5 particles ≥ 0.5 μm per 1cm² in a randomly selected 10cm² zone
  - Measurement tool: TSI LPC-100 laser particle counter, calibrated quarterly
  - Haze measurement: ≤ 0.15% (Haze-Gard Plus photometer)
4.2 All measurements recorded in the batch traveler (Form QF-3018).
4.3 Substrates failing cleanliness verification must be re-cleaned once. Second failure = reject and NCR initiation.

5. ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS
5.1 All cleaning steps must occur in an ISO Class 6 or better environment.
5.2 Temperature: 21°C ± 2°C
5.3 Relative humidity: 45% ± 10%
5.4 Personnel: Full cleanroom gown, gloves (nitrile, powder-free), face mask required.

6. INCOMING SUBSTRATE INSPECTION
6.1 All incoming substrates must pass incoming quality inspection (IQC) per customer spec CS-DG-001 before entering the cleaning process.
6.2 Substrates from Batch Materials Co. (SUP-003) lot 22B were subject to NCR-2024-141 (micro-inclusion finding). Consult QA before processing any lot received from SUP-003 after 2024-10-01 until CAPA-087 is closed.
