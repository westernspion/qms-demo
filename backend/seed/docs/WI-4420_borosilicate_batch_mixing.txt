1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This Work Instruction defines the batch formulation, incoming inspection, mixing sequence, and traceability requirements for borosilicate glass batch preparation at Apex Glass Technologies. Borosilicate glass produced per this instruction feeds the fiber preform process (WI-4455) and the FDM glass ribbon process (SOP-2024-019).

2. BATCH FORMULATION
Standard Borosilicate Glass Composition (weight percent):
  - Silicon dioxide (SiO2): 72.0% ± 0.5%   — Supplier: Silica Source Inc. (SUP-001)
  - Boron trioxide (B2O3): 13.0% ± 0.3%    — Supplier: Borate Specialists Ltd. (SUP-002)
  - Sodium oxide (Na2O): 9.0% ± 0.3%       — from sodium carbonate Na2CO3, Supplier: SUP-005
  - Aluminum oxide (Al2O3): 4.0% ± 0.2%    — Supplier: SUP-005
  - Potassium oxide (K2O): 2.0% ± 0.2%     — from potassium carbonate K2CO3, Supplier: SUP-005

Target total batch weight: 500 kg per melt. Scale tolerance: ±50g per component.

3. INCOMING MATERIAL INSPECTION
3.1 All raw materials must pass IQC before use in batch mixing:
  Component     | Test             | Method         | Acceptance Criteria
  SiO2          | Refractive index | ASTM C1349     | 1.4570 – 1.4585
  SiO2          | Purity           | XRF            | ≥ 99.8% SiO2
  B2O3          | Assay            | Titrimetry     | ≥ 99.5% B2O3
  Na2CO3        | Moisture         | LOI at 200°C   | ≤ 0.5% weight loss
  Al2O3         | Particle size    | Laser diffract | D50 = 5–15 μm

3.2 Lot traceability: Each raw material batch receives a unique lot number in the Material Management System (MMS). Lot number is recorded on the batch ticket and retained for minimum 7 years.

3.3 HOLD status materials: Materials under HOLD (per NCR or supplier quality issue) must not be issued to the batch mixing area. The MMS system will display a RED HOLD indicator for any held lot.
Current hold: SUP-001 lot #7842 is on HOLD per NCR-2024-156. Do not use until released by Quality.

4. MIXING SEQUENCE
Step 1 — Dry blending:
  a. Verify scale calibration (Form QF-0812, daily calibration log)
  b. Weigh SiO2 into mixing vessel. Record weight on batch ticket.
  c. Add B2O3. Mix 5 minutes at 30 RPM in ribbon blender.
  d. Add Na2CO3. Mix 3 minutes.
  e. Add Al2O3. Mix 3 minutes.
  f. Add K2CO3. Mix 5 minutes.
  g. Final blend: 10 minutes at 45 RPM. Visual inspection: no visible color streaks or unmixed lumps.

Step 2 — Moisture verification:
  Sample 100g from the top, middle, and bottom of the blend.
  Dry at 120°C for 2 hours. Weight loss ≤ 0.3% for all three samples.
  If any sample fails: discard batch, investigate moisture source, NCR required.

Step 3 — Charging to melting furnace:
  Pre-heat furnace to 1250°C before charging.
  Charge rate: 50 kg/15 minutes maximum to prevent thermal shock on furnace crown.
  Record charge completion time on furnace log (Form QF-0820).

5. TEMPERATURE PROFILE FOR MELTING
  1250°C → 1450°C: ramp at 5°C/min (homogenization phase, 2 hours)
  1450°C hold: 4 hours (fining phase — bubble elimination)
  1450°C → 1200°C: ramp down at 3°C/min (conditioning phase)
  Draw temperature: per SOP-2024-031 (2050°C at draw furnace, achieved by separate draw furnace, not batch furnace)

6. QUALITY CHECKS DURING MELT
6.1 Bubble count: sample via platinum tube extraction at 2h and 4h of fining. Acceptance: ≤ 2 bubbles/cm³ at 4h.
6.2 Refractive index of melt sample at 1200°C: measured by high-temperature refractometer. Target: 1.4578 ± 0.0008.
6.3 Viscosity at 1200°C: 10³ dPa·s ± 0.5 log units (measured by rotational viscometer SUL-VT3).

7. TRACEABILITY REQUIREMENTS
7.1 Each melt is assigned a unique Melt ID (format: MELT-YYYY-NNN).
7.2 The batch ticket links: Melt ID → raw material lot numbers → IQC results → batch ticket signer → furnace log.
7.3 Traceability must be maintained through fiber preform production and to finished fiber spool level.
